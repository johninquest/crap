# Simple Self‑Assessment Checklist for Small Businesses
## NIS2 & GDPR – Practical, Reusable, Easy to Understand

---

## Why this document exists (Thought Process & Intent)

Small businesses often struggle with NIS2 and GDPR because:
- the regulations are written for lawyers and regulators,
- guidance is scattered across long documents,
- and most checklists are either too technical or too abstract.

**The goal of this checklist is deliberately simple:**

- ✅ give small businesses a **quick snapshot of where they stand**
- ✅ allow them to **return to it anytime** (e.g. every 6–12 months)
- ✅ highlight **what still needs attention**, without panic
- ✅ focus on **reasonable, risk‑based behaviour**, not perfection

This is **not** a legal checklist  
This is **not** a certification tool  
This **is** a *decision‑support and awareness tool*

If a company can answer “Yes” to most questions, it is usually on the right path.  
If many answers are “No”, the checklist naturally points to the next priorities.

---

## How to use this checklist

- Answer each question with:
  - ✅ Yes
  - ⚠️ Partly / Needs work
  - ❌ No
- Revisit the checklist:
  - after IT changes,
  - after incidents,
  - or at least once or twice per year
- Treat every ❌ as **guidance**, not failure

---

# 1️⃣ NIS2 – Cybersecurity & Operational Resilience

**Purpose:**  
Are we reasonably protected against cyber incidents, and could we detect, respond to, and recover from them?

This section focuses on *practical resilience*, not deep technical security.

---

## A. Applicability & Responsibility

☐ Do we know whether NIS2 applies to our company (sector + size)?  
☐ Is someone clearly responsible for cybersecurity (internal or external)?  
☐ Is management aware that cybersecurity is a business responsibility, not only an IT one?

---

## B. Awareness of Critical Systems

☐ Do we know which systems are most important for our business to operate?  
☐ Do we understand what would hurt most if systems were unavailable for 1–3 days?  
☐ Have we thought about this at least once in the last 12 months?

---

## C. Basic Cybersecurity Hygiene

☐ Do employees use strong passwords and ideally multi‑factor authentication?  
☐ Are operating systems and software regularly updated?  
☐ Is access to systems limited to what people actually need?

---

## D. Incident Detection & Response

☐ Would we recognize a serious cyber incident (ransomware, phishing, outage)?  
☐ Do we know who to contact first (internal or external IT support)?  
☐ Do we know that serious incidents may need to be reported within **24 hours**?

---

## E. Business Continuity & Backups

☐ Are backups of important systems performed regularly?  
☐ Have we tested restoring a backup at least once?  
☐ Do we know how to continue basic operations if systems are temporarily unavailable?

---

## F. IT Service Providers & Suppliers

☐ Do we know which suppliers have access to our systems or data?  
☐ Have we discussed security or incident handling with key IT providers?  
☐ Do contracts at least mention security or incident notification?

---

### ✅ NIS2 Self‑Assessment Result

- **Mostly ✅ Yes** → Solid baseline for NIS2
- **Several ⚠️ / ❌** → Focus first on responsibility, backups, and incidents
- **Mostly ❌ No** → Immediate action recommended

---

# 2️⃣ GDPR – Data Protection & Privacy

**Purpose:**  
Do we process personal data lawfully, transparently, and securely?

This section focuses on *control and awareness*, not legal jargon.

---

## A. Personal Data Awareness

☐ Do we know what personal data we process (customers, employees, contacts)?  
☐ Do we know why we process each type of data (contract, legal duty, consent)?

---

## B. Basic Documentation

☐ Do we have a simple overview of what data we process and where it is stored?  
☐ Do we know which tools, systems, or service providers handle personal data?

*(This does not need to be complex – a simple list is usually enough for small businesses.)*

---

## C. Transparency & Information

☐ Do we provide a privacy notice (website, contracts, onboarding)?  
☐ Does it clearly explain what data we use and why?

---

## D. Rights of Individuals

☐ Do we know what to do if someone asks for their data?  
☐ Can we correct or delete data if requested?  
☐ Do we know the **30‑day deadline** for responding?

---

## E. Security of Personal Data

☐ Is access to personal data restricted to relevant staff only?  
☐ Are devices and systems protected by passwords or encryption?  
☐ Is personal data protected against accidental loss or leaks?

---

## F. Data Breaches

☐ Do we know what qualifies as a personal data breach?  
☐ Do we know who decides whether it must be reported?  
☐ Do we know that serious breaches must be reported within **72 hours**?

---

### ✅ GDPR Self‑Assessment Result

- **Mostly ✅ Yes** → Good level of GDPR control
- **Some ⚠️ / ❌** → Focus on transparency, security, and breach handling
- **Mostly ❌ No** → Elevated compliance and trust risk

---

# Final Notes for Small Businesses

- This checklist is **not about avoiding fines**
- It is about:
  - protecting the business,
  - building trust,
  - and being able to explain decisions

Regulators usually look for:
- awareness,
- structure,
- and continuous improvement — not perfection.

---

This checklist provides general guidance and does not replace legal or professional advice.  
Requirements may vary depending on country, sector, company size, and national implementation.
