# Everyday GenAI Reality Check (EU AI Act & Data Privacy)
## Simple Self-Assessment Checklist for Small Businesses & Freelancers

---

## Why this document exists

With the full enforcement of the EU AI Act in 2026, many small businesses and freelancers are unintentionally exposing themselves to massive legal and operational risks simply by using everyday GenAI tools (like ChatGPT, Claude, Midjourney, etc.).

**The goal of this checklist is:**
- ✅ to highlight the difference between "helpful AI" and "Shadow AI" data leaks
- ✅ to ensure you don't accidentally cross into the AI Act's "High-Risk" categories
- ✅ to establish basic human-in-the-loop safety nets

---

## How to use this checklist

- Answer each question with:
  - ✅ Yes
  - ⚠️ Partly / Needs work
  - ❌ No

---

# 1️⃣ Data Privacy & IP Leaks (The "Shadow AI" Problem)

**Purpose:** Are you feeding your company's secrets, or worse, your customers' personal data, to public AI models?

☐ Do your employees have a clear, written rule on what types of company/customer data are strictly forbidden from being pasted into public AI chatbots?
☐ Have you verified if the AI tools you pay for use your prompts and data to train their future public models?
☐ Do you know which AI tools your team is using on a daily basis (avoiding "Shadow IT")?

---

# 2️⃣ Transparency (The "Bot or Not" Rule)

**Purpose:** Article 50 of the AI Act requires deployers of certain AI systems to be transparent when users are interacting with AI or consuming AI-generated content.

☐ Do you clearly disclose to your customers when they are reading purely AI-generated content (e.g., automated support emails, blog posts, or marketing text)?
☐ If you use an AI chatbot on your website for customer service, is it immediately obvious to the user that they are not talking to a real human?

---

# 3️⃣ High-Risk "No-Go" Zones

**Purpose:** The AI Act classifies specific use cases as "High-Risk" (like HR, recruitment, and credit scoring), which explicitly require strict compliance, bias-checking, and logging.

☐ Are you absolutely sure you are NOT using AI tools to screen resumes, evaluate employee performance, or decide who gets hired?
☐ Do you avoid using AI to make automated decisions that significantly impact a customer's life, finances, or access to your services?

---

# 4️⃣ Human Oversight & Output Verification

**Purpose:** Article 14 requires "Human in the loop" oversight to prevent AI hallucinations or biases from causing harm and liability.

☐ Do you have a mandatory review process where a human must verify AI-generated technical, legal, or financial advice before it is sent to a client?
☐ Would you know who is legally liable in your business if an AI tool you use provides discriminatory or completely fabricated information to a customer?

---

### ✅ AI Reality Check Result

- **Mostly ✅ Yes** → You are using AI safely, maintaining oversight, and respecting the EU AI Act.
- **Several ⚠️ / ❌** → You are likely facing "Shadow AI" risks. Focus first on setting data-pasting rules and human-review policies.
- **Mostly ❌ No** → Immediate Action required. You may be violating GDPR by leaking customer data or stepping into High-Risk AI Act territory without compliance.
