# Cyber Risk Assessment Platform – Working Reference

## 1. Why This Exists (Founder Context)

Cyber risk has quietly become one of the top personal and business anxieties of the last decade.

**Market Context:**
- Attacks are more frequent, cheaper to execute, and harder to attribute
- Individuals fear identity theft, account takeovers, and financial loss
- Businesses fear downtime, liability, reputation damage, and denied insurance claims
- Cyber insurance products are growing fast — but requirements are unclear and inconsistent

**Shared Emotional Need (Individuals & SMEs):**
Clarity, reassurance, and confidence that the important bases are covered.

**Key Insight:**
Most people are not asking for perfect security. They are asking:
- "Am I exposed right now?"
- "What actually matters?"
- "If something happens, will I regret not doing more?"

**Mission:**
This platform exists to translate cyber risk into understandable, actionable guidance — without tools, agents, or fear-driven upselling.

## 2. Target Audiences (Summary)

### B2C – End Users
- Individuals with moderate digital exposure
- Fear-driven but time-poor
- Willing to pay small one-time fees for reassurance
- Can act as internal advocates at work

### B2B – Small & Medium Businesses
- SME owners
- Accidental IT / Ops managers
- Insurance-aware professionals (brokers, advisors)

**Note:** The same core assessment logic serves all groups. Language, depth, and outputs differ.

## 3. Must-Have Features (Comprehensive List)

### Core Assessment Engine (non-negotiable)
- **Question-based cyber risk assessment**
  - Plain-language questions (no technical jargon)
  - Modular structure (personal / company / device / behavior)
  - Deterministic scoring (transparent, explainable)

### Risk Scoring & Interpretation
- Simple overall score (e.g. Low / Medium / High)
Category-level scores (accounts, devices, backups, awareness)
Clear explanation of why a score is what it is

### Actionable Recommendations
- Prioritized actions (top 3–5 only)
Clear effort vs impact framing
No overwhelming checklists

### Incident-Aware Guidance
- "What to do first if something happens"
Common early mistakes to avoid
Insurance-relevant considerations

### Reporting & Artifacts
- Human-readable summary page
Shareable / printable outputs
Timestamped assessments

### Trust & Credibility Signals
- Clear scope (assessment, not protection)
Conservative claims
Professional tone

### Lightweight B2C Flows
- Quick checks (3–5 minutes)
- Knowledge quizzes
- Life-event/device-triggered assessments

### B2B-Ready Structure
- Company-level assessment mode
- Repeat assessments over time
- Consistent framework suitable for advisors/insurers

## 4. Top 3 Features To Focus On Initially (MVP Focus)

### 1. Lightweight Risk Assessment + Scoring

**Why:**
- Core value proposition
- Validates whether people care
- Reusable across all personas

**What it includes:**
- Short questionnaire
- Simple score
- Immediate feedback

### 2. Clear, Confidence-Building Recommendations

**Why:**
- Scores alone are not valuable
- Users pay for guidance, not numbers

**What it includes:**
- Top risks
- Concrete next steps
- Language that reduces anxiety

### 3. Incident-First Framing

**Why:**
- This drives memorability
- This enables B2C → B2B spillover
- This aligns with insurance reality

**What it includes:**
- "If this happens, do this first"
- Early-response checklist
- Shareable guidance



## 5. Validation-First Build Strategy

### Principles
- Build the smallest thing that creates reassurance
- Validate fear, not feature depth
- Charge early, even small amounts

### Early Validation Paths
- Free short quiz → paid detailed result
- One-time B2C checks (€2–€7)
- Direct outreach to SME owners for feedback

## 6. B2C Pitch (Buyer Psychology Driven)

### Core Emotional Drivers
- Fear of the unknown
- Desire for reassurance
- Avoidance of embarrassment or regret

### Positioning
"A quick, clear check to understand your cyber risk — and what actually matters right now."

### Messaging Pillars
- Fast
- No tools to install
- Plain language
- Calm, not alarmist

Cyber threats change fast. Most people don't know if their habits and devices are still safe.

In a few minutes, get a clear picture of your risk, what you're doing well, and what's worth fixing — without jargon or scare tactics.

## 8. B2C → B2B Spillover (Intentional Design)

The platform is designed so that:
- Individuals gain language and confidence
- Guidance naturally applies to workplaces
- Users can recommend it under stress

### Key Mechanisms
- Incident guidance usable at work
- Language bridges (personal vs company)
- One-click transition to company assessment

It is a decision and clarity tool.

**If users walk away thinking:**
- "I understand my risk better"
- "I know what matters"
- "I feel calmer and more prepared"

Then the product is doing its job.
